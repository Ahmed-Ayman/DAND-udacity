(1) What is the independent variable? What is the dependent variable?

  • the independent variable is the test condition that is the congruent condition or the incongruent.
  • the dependent variable is the time it to takes to name the colors.

(2) What is an appropriate set of hypotheses for this task? Specify your null and alternative hypotheses, and clearly define any notation used. Justify your choices.

finding whether the mean difference between the two datasets is statically significant.

$\mu_c$ is the average time taken to name the Congruent words

$\mu_{in}$ is the average time taken to name the inCongruent words

the Null hypothesis (H0): The average time taken to name the color of the incongruent $\mu_{in}$ and the congruent words $\mu_c$ is just the same $$ \mu_{in} -\mu_c = 0 $$

the Alternative Hypothesis (H1): The average time taken to name the color of the incongurent words $\mu_{in}$ and the congruent words $\mu_c$ isn't the same and significantly different $$ \mu_{in} -\mu_c \neq 0 $$

here we will be using the T-test because we don't have the population parameters, we have a small sample hence we can use the T-table and we just have samples that we will be using to estimate the population parameter that is the difference between the time averages in each condition.

on the other hand if we had the Population paramters like the mean and the STD, we could be using the z-test instead.

here we performed a dependent(paired) T-test because the same sample has performed the test under varying conditions that is the congruent and incongruent words.
